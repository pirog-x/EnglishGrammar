# natural disasters, weather idioms

## natural disasters

| Nr | Word              | Definition                                                                                     |
|----|-------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | avalanche         | a mass of snow, ice, and debris that rapidly slides down a mountain slope                      |
| 2  | drought           | a long period of time during which no rain falls                                               |
| 3  | earthquake        | a sudden shaking of the ground caused by tectonic plate movements                              |
| 4  | flood             | a large amount of water covering an area that is usually dry                                   |
| 5  | hurricane         | a violent storm with very strong winds, especially in the western Atlantic Ocean               |
| 6  | landslide         | a mass of earth, rock, etc. that falls down the slope of a mountain or a cliff                 |
| 7  | tsunami           | an extremely large wave in the sea caused, for example, by an earthquake                       |
| 8  | tornado           | a violent storm with very strong winds that move in a circle                                   |
| 9  | volcanic eruption | the release of magma, gases, and ash from a volcano                                            |
| 10 | wildfire          | a very big fire that spreads quickly and burns natural areas like woods, forests and grassland |

## Idioms

| Nr | Word                          | Definition                                                                 |
|----|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1  | be a breeze                   | to be easy, especially unexpectedly easy                                   |
| 2  | come rain or shine            | whatever happens; no matter what the situation is                          |
| 3  | save up for a rainy day       | to save money for a time when it might be needed unexpectedly              |
| 4  | the calm before the storm     | a brief moment of peace or quiet before a busy or difficult period arrives |
| 5  | under the weather             | feeling unwell or ill                                                      |
