# asking for and giving opinion, agreeing and disagreeing, opinion adjectives

# Giving opinion
- I'm convinced that...
- It seems to me that...
- From my perspective, ...
- Basically / Personally, I believe / think / feel ...

# Asking for opinion
- How do you see...?
- How do you feel about...?
- What's your take on...?
- What are your views on...?

# Agreeing
- I couldn't agree more.
- I'm on the same page with you.
- I'm with you on this.
- That's exactly how I feel.

# Disagreeing
- I beg to differ.
- That's not how I see it.
- I have a different take on this.
- I'm not so sure about that.


# Dialogue: Hybrid Work Model Discussion

**Andrew:** **What's your take on** the hybrid work model many companies are adopting now?

**Ben:** **Personally, I believe** it's a step in the right direction. It allows employees to effectively juggle between remote work and being physically present in the office. What's more, **I'm convinced that** the hybrid approach can boost productivity.

**Andrew:** Well, Ben, **I beg to differ.** **It seems to me that** the hybrid work model might blur the lines between work and personal life, making it harder to disconnect when needed.

**Ben:** Honestly, **that's not how I see it.** **From my perspective,** this model promotes a healthier work-life integration by giving employees more control over their schedules.

**Andrew:** **I'm with you on this point,** though. **Basically, I think** this model grants employees greater authority over their schedules, which can lead to a more productive and satisfied workforce.

**Ben:** Exactly! And it's also a good way to attract and retain top talent, as in today's competitive job market, all employees are looking for companies that offer flexible work arrangements.

**Andrew:** **I couldn't agree more,** Ben! The hybrid model is undoubtedly a great way to meet that demand. I'm starting to see the benefits of the hybrid work model now.
